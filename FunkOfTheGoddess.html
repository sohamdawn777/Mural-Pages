The Funk of the Goddess

In an old, crumbling by-lane of North Kolkata, spray cans have reinvented mythology. A goddess-like figure with multiple arms holds a mic, while graffiti tags swirl around her. A Royal Enfield parked nearby makes it look like the goddess herself might ride off any moment.

This mural embodies Kolkata’s underground hip-hop and street dance culture. Since the early 2000s, young artists here began mixing graffiti with Bengali iconography—Durga not as a traditional warrior but as a rapper, DJ, or breaker. The message is clear: divinity today wears sneakers and flows to beats.

But there’s more—this is rebellion. Once, colonial Calcutta saw its walls covered in political slogans, rally cries, and protest art. Now, the same walls bear the energy of youth who are no longer just chanting slogans but rapping them, tagging them, and performing them. It’s a remix of tradition and resistance.
