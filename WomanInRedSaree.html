The Woman in Red Saree

On the cracked wall of a modest house in North Kolkata, a mural blooms with vibrant contradictions—half green, half pink, trees and houses merging around the figure of a woman in a red saree. She sits calmly, her yellow skin and surreal face suggesting she is more than just a mortal. She could be the city itself—Kolkata personified as a goddess.

Historically, Kolkata has always been depicted as feminine—whether in colonial literature describing her as a “Black Town” mothering resistance, or in Durga Puja pandals where women embody the goddess. The houses beneath her symbolize old para culture, while the trees whisper of a city once known as the “City of Palaces” but now also deeply tied to environmental battles. This mural is a quiet reclaiming of identity: the woman stands as a bridge between the rural heart of Bengal and the chaotic urban sprawl of Kolkata.
